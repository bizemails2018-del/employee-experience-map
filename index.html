"Every Interaction Leaves a Mark"

"Employees don't experience your intentions. They experience your patterns."

Most leaders evaluate themselves based on their intentions. But employees don't experience intentions — they experience what happens repeatedly.

This exercise asks you to step out of your own perspective and examine your leadership through the eyes of one specific employee. As you move through each touchpoint, look for patterns. Do your answers reveal strong communication but weak follow-through? Do you show up well when things are calm but struggle under pressure?

The goal isn't perfection. The goal is awareness.

Leadership blind spots rarely show up as dramatic failures. They show up as small, repeated moments that slowly shape how employees feel about their manager, their team, and their workplace. Be honest — only then will this exercise be worth your time.
